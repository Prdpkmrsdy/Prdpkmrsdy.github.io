VHF-band Low Noise Amplifier (125–160 MHz)

Designed a VHF LNA PCB targeting stable gain and low noise performance across the 125–160 MHz band. The design uses a PGA-103+ RF amplifier stage with 50Ω matching, tuned LC filtering, clean bias regulation, and RF-aware layout practices to reduce parasitics and preserve noise figure.

RF Performance Targets

  • Targeted ~18 dB gain and low noise operation across 125–160 MHz
  • 50Ω input/output matching strategy for measurement-ready integration
  • RF routing, grounding, and component placement tuned to reduce parasitic coupling

Front-End Architecture

  • PGA-103+ amplifier stage with AC coupling and controlled biasing
  • Tuned LC filtering (band-limited response and out-of-band rejection)
  • TLV1117-based regulation and local decoupling for clean bias rails

Design Practices

  • Short RF traces, controlled return paths, and ground stitching near RF sections
  • Dedicated power filtering and local bypass capacitors near active device pins
  • Component footprint selection and placement to preserve Q and reduce loss

Validation Approach

  • Verification plan built around impedance matching and gain stability checks
  • Measurement-ready interfaces for NanoVNA/TinySA-style validation workflows
  • Iterative tuning of filter and matching values based on real-world response
