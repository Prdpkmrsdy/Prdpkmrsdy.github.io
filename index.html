About

I am a Graduate Research Engineer at Colorado State University, currently working at the CHILL Radar Facility on radar system development, hardware bring-up, system integration, and validation. My work focuses on instrumentation, calibration checks, and maintaining reliable test setups for operational research systems. My engineering foundation was built through hands-on roles in automotive systems, electric motor manufacturing, and Formula Student EV programs, where I served as Electrical Team Lead and Electrical Safety Officer. I was later invited by SAE – Formula Bharat to join the Electrical Inspection Crew for the Formula Electric race, strengthening my understanding of electrical safety standards and compliance. I subsequently worked at an EV startup, kWh Bikes, where I led powertrain sizing, wiring harness design, and DC-DC converter development, supporting the transition from prototype mules to pilot production through AIS and ARAI compliance. At Chara Technologies, I worked on rare-earth-free electric powertrains (3–70 kW), focusing on FOC tuning, motor and inverter validation, high-power dynamometer testing, and vehicle integration. Across these roles, I have led structured validation workflows, supported hardware bring-up, and resolved system-level issues with a strong emphasis on safety, repeatability, and production-ready engineering.

Demo Videos

Custom Startup Sequence and data logging setup Demo

A 22 kW electric powertrain, powered by a 24 kWh high-energy-density lithium-ion battery pack, was retrofitted into a Tata Ace commercial vehicle to validate the complete EV powertrain system. The setup includes the development of a four-wheeler wiring harness, a dedicated cooling system for both the traction motor and accumulator, and the integration of a heat exchanger for thermal management. A centralized control network was designed and implemented, enabling seamless integration of the motor controller and associated vehicle subsystems. The demonstration also features a custom user interface and fault-indication switches, along with a TI-controller-based CAN data logger for real-time monitoring and diagnostics. The demo showcases the system startup sequence, powertrain activation, and drivetrain rotation in both clockwise and anti-clockwise directions, validating functional operation and control logic of the electric drivetrain.

Project Demo

The project develops a machine-learning–based surrogate model to predict spatial thermal and power distributions in VLSI physical design using floorplan-level features. Physical design data is extracted from OpenROAD, rasterized into fixed-resolution grids, and used to train a convolutional neural network (CNN) to regress temperature maps generated by the HotSpot thermal simulator. The trained model enables fast, low-latency thermal estimation during early physical design stages and is optimized for embedded deployment using INT8 TensorFlow Lite on ARM platforms, demonstrating practical feasibility for ML-assisted, power- and thermal-aware VLSI design workflows..
